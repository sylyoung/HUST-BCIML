# CSP-Net

**Axis:** Network (backbone) &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Paradigm:** MI &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Dataset:** BNCI2014001 (cross-subject LOSO)

**Stage configuration:** `EA · no-aug · CSPNet · Linear · ERM`

## Mechanism
A standard EEGNet whose depthwise spatial convolution is initialized with Common Spatial Pattern filters estimated from the EA-aligned source data and then frozen — the CSP-Net-2 configuration — so the spatial layer is the CSP solution while the temporal and separable convolutions train normally. It injects classical, well-understood CSP spatial filtering into the network as a structural prior, and leads the backbone axis.

## Result — 3 seeds (1, 2, 3)

| Metric | Value |
|---|--:|
| Accuracy (mean ± std across seeds) | 76.13 ± 0.85 |
| Δ vs baseline (EA-EEGNet, 72.07) | +4.06 |
| Reference range (expected band) | 72–82 |

Accuracy is the mean over seeds; ± is the standard deviation *across* seeds (a reproducibility figure, not the cross-subject spread). The reference range is the published / expected band on this dataset.

## Provenance
- **Paper / source:** Jiang, Meng, Chen, Xu & Wu, Knowledge-Based Systems 305:112668, 2024 (CSP-Net), doi 10.1016/j.knosys.2024.112668; github.com/jxgogo/CSP-Net — CSP-Net-2 (spatial-layer replacement), fixed CSP filters
- **Implementation:** Adapted from github.com/jxgogo/CSP-Net (Jiang et al. 2024, Knowledge-Based Systems) onto the Backbone ABC as CSP-Net-2-fix: the depthwise spatial-conv weights are overwritten with mne CSP filters (average-power, epoch covariance) computed by a data-dependent init hook on the training split, then frozen; the eight CSP filters are tiled to fill the spatial-conv output channels, matching the source. CSP-Net-1 (a prepended CSP layer) is the non-default variant. See the upstream repository for its original license terms.

## Note
backbone axis, lab method. EEGNet whose depthwise spatial conv is initialized with CSP filters from the EA-aligned source and then frozen (CSP-Net-2-fix). Leads the backbone table: +4.06 over EEGNet (72.07), above deep DBConformer (76.03). The paper's own MI2C (2-class 2a, 9 subjects, 144 trials/subject) is this benchmark's BNCI2014001; it reports CSP-Nets consistently improving over their CNN backbones in both within- and cross-subject MI (within-subject 4-class 2a: EEGNet 63.50 -> 71.91), with the cross-subject gains figure-encoded and smaller than within-subject — the measured +4.06 is consistent. CSP filters are estimated on raw epochs but overwrite the post-temporal-conv depthwise layer, an approximation the paper adopts.
